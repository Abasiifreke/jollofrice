My Favourite meal

My favourite meal is jollof rice. Jollof rice is a rice dish that is popular in West African countries, and all over the world. It is a one-pot dish where rice is slowly cooked in a rich tomato sauce, herbs, and spices base. It is also sometimes referred to as Jolof rice. There are different variations of West African Jollof rice including the Nigerian jollof rice, Senegalese, Liberia, and Ghana jollof rice. According to Wikipedia, Jollof derives its name from the name of the Wolof people. In Senegal and Gambia, it is referred to in Wolof as ceebu jÃ«n or benachin. In French-speaking areas, it is called riz au gras. List of ingredients Measurement
Rice 3 cups
tomato paste 2 satchet
chicken whole chicken
pepper to taste
salt to taste
onion 2 bulbs
knorr cubes 3
thyme 2 teaspoons
curry 2 teaspoons

Jollof rice recipe

  • Place a pot on heat, add oil into the pot, then add in sliced onions. Sautee the onions till they are fragrant and translucent. This should take about 2 minutes.
  • Next, add in the tomato paste. Fry for 5-10 minutes till reduced in quantity and doesn't taste tart.
  • Add in the pepper,curry powder, dried thyme, knor cubes, and salt. Fry till it is dark red and no longer raw. It will be reduced in quantity, thick and the oil floats to the top of the sauce. This takes about 10 - 15 minutes.
  • Then add in the chicken and boiling water. Stir, add in the washed rice. Cover the pot tightly and cook the rice on low heat till it's almost tender. (About 25 minutes).
  • Cover the pot and continue to steam the rice till it's tender.
  • Serve and enjoy!
